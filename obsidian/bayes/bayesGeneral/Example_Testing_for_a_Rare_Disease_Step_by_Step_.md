

Title: Example: Testing for a Rare Disease (Step-by-Step)
Content: Disease prevalence P(D)=0.01. Test sensitivity P(T|D)=0.95, specificity P(T^c|D^c)=0.95. P(D|T) = [P(T|D)P(D)]/P(T) = (0.95×0.01)/0.059 ≈ 0.161. Only 16.1% chance of disease despite positive test!
